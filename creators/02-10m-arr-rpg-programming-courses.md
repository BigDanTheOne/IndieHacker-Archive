# Hitting $10M ARR with RPG-style programming courses

**Author:** James Fleischmann
**Date:** January 6, 2026
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/creators/hitting-10m-arr-with-rpg-style-programming-courses-b1JEom0xSuVU4EIvPfdf

---

Lane Wagner was dissatisfied with existing online education options for backend developers, so he created Boot.dev, an RPG-inspired educational platform that generates $10 million in annual recurring revenue.

## Background

Wagner, a backend engineer experienced in JavaScript, TypeScript, Python, and Go, felt that traditional in-person education was inefficient while online offerings lacked quality. Backend development particularly suffered from inadequate educational resources. He launched Boot.dev to address this gap, gradually transforming it into an immersive programming education platform featuring interactive tooling and RPG mechanics.

## Product Development

The platform began with Markdown-based content and code examples. The technical foundation includes Go, PostgreSQL, Kubernetes, Docker, Google Cloud Platform, Nuxt, TypeScript, and Cloudflare. The company now collaborates with external authors while maintaining rigorous quality standards through in-house editors and video animators. As Wagner notes: "The future of online learning is quality first, not quantity first."

## Monetization Strategy

All course content remains freely accessible. Revenue derives from premium interactive features including Boots (an AI assistant), in-browser code execution, CLI tools, completion certificates, and unlockable spellbooks and solutions. Users access these through monthly or yearly subscriptions.

## Marketing Approach

Minimal marketing occurred initially. YouTube drives the majority of user acquisition through organic content and influencer collaborations. While blogging supports SEO efforts, Wagner emphasizes that "video builds trust." The company has achieved success with direct-to-consumer advertising on YouTube, Google, and Meta platforms.

## Growth Philosophy

Wagner maintains a lean operation with 13 team members, hiring only when he is certain roles will be effectively utilized. He prioritizes profitable, incremental growth and warns against overhiring. A critical piece of advice: "Do NOT try to sell to other indie hackers. Find a different customer base."

## Future Vision

Wagner aspires to position Boot.dev as the premier destination for coding and technology education globally, though he acknowledges the substantial work required to expand course offerings across all desired subjects.
