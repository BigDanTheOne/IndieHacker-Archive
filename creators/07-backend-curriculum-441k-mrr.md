# Growing a back-end development curriculum from a side project into a $441k MRR business

**Author:** James Fleischmann
**Date:** November 20, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/creators/growing-a-backend-development-curriculum-from-a-side-project-into-a-441k-mrr-business-XZ5V3c4pAFEygTxZv83N

---

Lane Wagner founded boot.dev after recognizing a gap in online backend development education. As a former backend engineer and engineering manager, he built the platform to address what he saw as a missing resource.

## Business Model

Boot.dev offers free content with paid interactive features including answer verification, AI mentorship, solution viewing, and gamification elements like achievements and quests. The platform currently generates $441k in monthly recurring revenue through yearly and monthly memberships.

## Growth Strategy

Wagner initially pursued SEO through blogging but found YouTube to be the breakthrough channel. He launched the Backend Banter podcast and main YouTube channel, deliberately avoiding direct monetization to prioritize audience trust and platform growth.

## Key Lessons

Wagner emphasizes focusing energy on a single growth channel rather than spreading across multiple platforms. He notes that "They aren't free. They're very expensive in terms of your time."

## Direct-to-Consumer Success

Rather than pursuing B2B sales, Wagner built a D2C business, cautioning entrepreneurs against targeting indie hackers as customers since they "don't have money, and they have a ton of time."

## Content Excellence

Strong copywriting and quality content drove success. Wagner stresses the importance of regular updates -- daily and weekly improvements rather than annual refreshes -- and maintaining the highest standards for course quality.

## Future Plans

Boot.dev plans to expand into TypeScript, Go, data engineering, and DevOps while refining the user interface.
