# How a college student reached $64,000/mo in 6 months by being an AI first mover

**Author:** Channing Allen
**Date:** May 21, 2023
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/how-a-college-student-reached-64-000-mo-in-6-months-by-being-an-ai-first-mover-ba7981f6e1

---

Yasser Elsaid, a fourth-year college student, transformed his side project into a revenue-generating business by capitalizing on the ChatGPT momentum. His company, Chatbase.co, reached $64,000 in monthly recurring revenue within six months of launch.

## Background and Motivation

Originally from Egypt, Elsaid moved to Canada for his undergraduate studies. Initially pursuing the conventional path of securing internships at major tech companies like Tesla and Meta, he found the corporate environment lacking excitement despite surface-level perks. This realization prompted him to pivot toward entrepreneurship after listening to success stories on the Indie Hackers podcast.

## Product Development

Chatbase began as a "ChatGPT for PDFs" tool, allowing users to upload documents and interact with their content. Elsaid developed the MVP in approximately two months, launching on February 2, 2023, to just 16 Twitter followers. His initial tweet gained significant traction, and he immediately abandoned university commitments to focus entirely on the opportunity. He ultimately failed two courses but considered the trade-off worthwhile.

The technical foundation uses React, Next.js, and Supabase for the frontend, combined with OpenAI's API, Langchain, and Pinecone for AI capabilities, plus Stripe for payment processing.

## Growth and Marketing Strategy

The product achieved viral status through organic social media sharing and influencer attention. Early marketing channels included Product Hunt, relevant subreddit communities, Indie Hackers participation, and AI directory submissions. Traffic remained stable following the initial launch surge.

Revenue progression demonstrated exponential growth:
- February 7: $0
- February 11: $400
- February 16: $900
- February 28: $3,000
- March 15: $10,000
- May 13: $64,000

The subscription model features four pricing tiers, with the $399 monthly plan generating the majority of revenue.

## Future Outlook and Advice

Elsaid acknowledges significant challenges ahead, including intense competition and uncertainty about OpenAI's strategic direction. He plans to expand the team to five or six employees while continuing feature development based on customer feedback.

For aspiring entrepreneurs, he recommends:
- Launching MVPs immediately rather than building waitlists
- Leveraging multiple product launches when making significant updates
- Building publicly through social platforms like Twitter
- Engaging actively with entrepreneurial communities

His key insight emphasizes finding markets with "SOME competition," as zero competition suggests either exceptional vision or nonexistent demand, while excessive competition creates unsustainable pricing pressure.
