# From Our Desks: Leaderboards and Newsroom Ambitions

**Authors:** Courtland Allen, Katie Hignett, Stephen Flanders, Channing Allen, Darko Gjorgjievski, and James Fleischmann
**Date:** November 3, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/starting-up/from-our-desks-leaderboards-and-newsroom-ambitions-GvYMphGS3Vba1g0O2UDM

---

This weekly column features perspectives from the Indie Hackers team on current challenges and developments in their platform and the startup ecosystem.

## Channing Allen

Reports on expanding the newsletter program from three to five issues weekly. When illness forced him to step back from coordinating the newsroom, his team autonomously managed story assignments, newsgathering, and editing. The team successfully met their ambitious milestone by taking initiative without micromanagement, allowing the focus to shift toward original reporting.

## Courtland Allen

Discusses reconsidering the product leaderboard strategy. After observing that "launching isn't something you can do over and over without being annoying," he determined that launch-based leaderboards lack scalability compared to blogging and social media. He redesigned the leaderboard to feature content-driven updates rather than product launches, creating what he describes as a "build in public leaderboard" where community members share progress updates.

## Katie Hignett

Reflects on Twitter/X's rising API costs -- doubling from $100 to $200 monthly. She notes building simple projects on the platform is becoming prohibitively expensive. She removed the X app from her phone, finding that "if it's work, it should stay on a laptop."

## Stephen Flanders

Shares frustration about developing on WordPress while building his plugin Raffle Leader. He describes the experience as challenging, citing slow approval processes and ongoing ecosystem drama surrounding leadership.

## Darko Gjorgjievski

Observes that "best practices become worst practices" in digital marketing over time. He highlights articles covering email marketing evolution, unicorn startups, top creators, and AI competition between major tech companies.

## James Fleischmann

Interviews founders who left corporate careers due to meaninglessness. Both discovered that witnessing the tangible impact of their work motivated their entrepreneurial journey, suggesting impact -- not just financial freedom -- drives people toward independent ventures.
