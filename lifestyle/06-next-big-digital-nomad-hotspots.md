# Are these the next big digital nomad hotspots?

**Author:** Katie Hignett
**Date:** October 23, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/lifestyle/are-these-the-next-big-digital-nomad-hotspots-yZIRFPGDRrDYlIJpgUKi

---

The digital nomad lifestyle continues gaining momentum as travelers seek alternatives to overcrowded destinations like Bali and Bangkok. Travel companies Expedia and Booking.com report surging interest in lesser-known locations, with airlines establishing new routes to remote areas including Greenland and Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia.

## Emerging Destinations

### Naha, Okinawa, Japan

Offers warm weather and a new digital nomad visa allowing six-month stays for those earning $65,000+ annually. The city provides good infrastructure and activities like surfing, though internet speeds average only 31 Mbps and English proficiency is limited.

### Faro, Portugal

Sits at #56 on Nomads.com and costs approximately $2,000 monthly -- significantly cheaper than Lisbon's $3,200. United Airlines launches direct New York flights in 2025. The coastal city features co-working spaces but limited cafe wi-fi for laptop workers.

### Koh Lanta, Thailand

Hosts a tight-knit November-April nomad community. A recently completed bridge connection to Krabi promises increased accessibility. The island offers beaches and hiking alongside the KoHub co-working space, though power outages occur occasionally.

### Fukuoka, Japan

Runs government-sponsored co-living programs hosting hundreds of remote workers, featuring events and wellness activities, though mediocre wi-fi and higher living costs present challenges.
