# "Earned $1.2M, launched 5 startups" in 2024: indie hackers share their year-in-reviews

**Author:** Michal Kankowski
**Date:** January 1, 2025
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/lifestyle/earned-1-2m-launched-5-startups-in-2024-indie-hackers-share-their-year-in-reviews-g61Lu08M3otlLESYu9m9

---

From crushing exits to burnt-out laptops, here's what this year taught us.

2024 proved transformative for the indie hacking community, showcasing both remarkable achievements and sobering challenges.

## Success Stories

Several founders achieved significant financial milestones: Nico Jeannen generated $350K from exits and projects, gaining freedom to travel internationally. Marc Louvion earned $1.2M while launching five startups, amassing 87K X followers and 104K YouTube subscribers. He also invested $5,000 weekly in index funds and exercised 340+ days annually.

Josh Mohrer, founder of Wave AI, scaled his SaaS from 200 to 22,000 paid subscriptions, generating $450K monthly recurring revenue -- accomplishing this as a first-time programmer using AI assistance. Ankit grew from $570 monthly to $200K annual revenue and launched 70+ free tools. Idan Masas, at age 20, increased his X presence from 700 to 5,000 followers while achieving $60K SaaS revenue. JK Molina nearly doubled his earnings from $630K to $1.23M, while reducing his workday from 12 to 4 hours.

## The Hidden Costs

However, success carried hidden costs. Nico experienced burnout and depression from overwork, developing chronic neck and back issues from excessive laptop time. Jason Leow described 2024 as filled with "dead ends" -- failed consulting projects and unsuccessful product launches -- though he reframed these setbacks as directional guidance.

## The Overarching Lesson

The overarching lesson emphasized balancing relentless effort with sustainable practices. Hard work creates opportunities, but timing and mindset prove equally crucial. Experts stressed prioritizing breaks, pursuing personal interests, and maintaining community connections to combat isolation and burnout. The takeaway: success requires both dedication and intentional self-care for long-term viability.
