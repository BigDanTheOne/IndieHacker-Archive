# Building a $100M ARR Portfolio of Products

**Founder:** Tim Schumacher
**Product:** saas.group
**Revenue:** $100M+ ARR ($8.3M+ MRR)
**Source:** [IndieHackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/erendy8MDnGpBEP0F2GN)

---

## Overview

25+ independent SaaS brands across 30 countries, ~400 employees.

## The Journey

Tim Schumacher transitioned from building Sedo.com (a domain marketplace he ran for a decade) to identifying a gap in the market: talented founders were burning out, stuck in their own businesses, or unable to scale despite strong products. Simultaneously, acquisitions frequently failed because acquirers didn't understand how to operate the products properly.

"I was far better at scaling than building from scratch," Tim explains, leading him to combine his scaling expertise with founders' need for "trusted long-term homes."

## The saas.group Model

Rather than treating individual products as the core business, Tim treats the acquisition and operating playbook itself as the product. The portfolio includes established brands like:

- **GitTower.com** -- graphical Git client
- **ScraperAPI.com** -- web scraping for developers
- **Ayrshare.com** -- social media connectivity
- **Prerender.io** -- website visibility and AI at scale

saas.group typically acquires companies generating between $2-$5M in annual recurring revenue, then applies a repeatable operating engine centered on small, autonomous teams supported by shared central services for marketing, DevOps, and finance.

## Core Operating Principles

**Bootstrapper Alignment:** Focusing on founders with bootstrapper mentality creates efficient, frugal teams with deep product ownership -- critical for sustainable growth.

**Simple, Maintainable Stacks:** Rather than enforcing standardization, teams maintain diverse but maintainable technology choices. Technical diligence prioritizes "code quality, maintainability, and debt rather than specific technologies."

**Autonomous Teams with Shared Services:** Small, high-performance teams consistently outperform larger organizations when given autonomy while receiving central support.

## Growth and Scaling

Growth relies on "execution over innovation." The company employs standard SaaS strategies: content marketing and SEO, search and social advertising, product-led growth approaches. However, reputation serves as the primary acquisition driver.

## Product Evolution Examples

**AddSearch Pivot:** When classic website search became less critical, the team successfully relaunched as "AI Answers," an AI-powered answer engine, restoring ARR growth.

**Keyword.com Enhancement:** Facing declining ARR from traditional SEO monitoring, the team added brand visibility tracking across major AI search platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini), reigniting growth.

**Infrastructure Efficiency:** Prerender.io saw an 80% AWS cost reduction post-acquisition, adding hundreds of thousands to the bottom line without revenue changes.

## Key Lessons

**Preparation Matters:** Understanding numbers, talking with real users early, and treating acquisition preparation like product-market fit fundamentally improves outcomes.

**Avoid Comparison Traps:** Focus on building something people genuinely want rather than chasing unicorn narratives.

**Prioritize Systems Over Hype:** Long-term sustainability beats chasing hype cycles. Consistency and operational discipline compound more effectively than individual growth optimizations.
