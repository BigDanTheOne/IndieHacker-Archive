# Hitting $10M ARR with RPG-Style Programming Courses

**Founder:** Lane Wagner
**Product:** Boot.dev
**Revenue:** $10M ARR ($833K+ MRR)
**Source:** [IndieHackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/b1JEom0xSuVU4EIvPfdf)

---

## The Story

Lane Wagner, a backend engineer experienced in JavaScript, TypeScript, Python, and Go, created Boot.dev to address a gap in online programming education. He felt that "in-person or live education was too inefficient, but online education wasn't a great experience," particularly for backend development, which remained underserved.

The platform evolved from simple Markdown-based content into "the most immersive programming education platform available" through interactive tooling and RPG-themed gamification.

## Technical Stack

Go, PostgreSQL, Kubernetes, Docker, Google Cloud Platform, Nuxt, TypeScript, and Cloudflare.

## Business Model

Freemium approach: all course content remains free to access, but interactive features are monetized through monthly or yearly subscriptions. Paid capabilities include:

- "Boots," an AI assistant mascot
- In-browser code writing and execution
- CLI tools
- Completion certificates
- Unlockable reference materials and solutions

## Growth Strategy

Organic channels drove early momentum. The company attracted users primarily through YouTube, both via organic educational content and collaborations with influencers. Lane noted that video "builds trust" more effectively than written content.

The fact that all content is free to read and watch proved "actually crazy helpful for getting a lot of free traction and organic reach."

## Quality-First Philosophy

The team works with external authors and maintains rigorous internal editing alongside video animation. "The future of online learning is quality first, not quantity first."

## Key Advice

"Do NOT try to sell to other indie hackers. Find a different customer base." The builder community represents a saturated, small market compared to the massive backend developer audience seeking legitimate education.

- 13 employees
