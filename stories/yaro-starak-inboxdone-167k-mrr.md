# Building an Agency from $0 to $2M/yr

**Founder:** Yaro Starak
**Product:** InboxDone
**Revenue:** $167K MRR ($2M annually)
**Source:** [IndieHackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/zgmjCIo91womyJaQQTLM)

---

## Overview

InboxDone is an executive assistant service specializing in email management and schedule coordination. 80 contractors, mostly North American.

## Origin Story

Yaro discovered the concept in the early 2000s while running an editing agency. He hired a friend to manage his inbox and experienced transformative relief. He launched InboxDone in 2016, partnering with Claire. First validation: a simple email to Yaro's newsletter yielded five inquiries, three calls, two customers at $1,000/month.

## Business Model

- Entry plan: $1,995/month
- Full-time plan: $4,995/month
- Contractor pay: $17-$35/hour
- Profit margins: 0-40% historically
- Churn: Below 4% throughout

## Growth Milestones

- Year 1: $10K/month using existing audience
- Hit plateau relying solely on personal following
- Breakthrough when focusing entirely on one business

## Key Lessons

**On Validation:** "Paying customers are the only true validation."

**On Focus:** Committing fully to InboxDone immediately increased momentum despite minimal operational changes.

**On Market Selection:** Choose industries with persistent problems and favorable margins.

**On Channels:**
- Google (paid/organic): Small but consistent
- LinkedIn ads: Equally reliable
- Referrals: Became dominant as client base expanded
- Failed: Conference sponsorships, most podcasts, YouTube ads

**On Team Compensation:** Premium pay ($17-$35/hour) attracts quality talent essential for managing sensitive inbox access.
