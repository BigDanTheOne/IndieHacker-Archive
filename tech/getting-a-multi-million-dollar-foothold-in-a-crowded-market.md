# Getting a multi-million-dollar foothold in a crowded market

**Author:** James Fleischmann
**Date:** January 14, 2026
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/IKfGcwhlcGQDnjExin8A

---

Ruben Gamez founded SignWell, an electronic signature platform processing over ten million signed documents. The company generates several million in annual recurring revenue with tens of thousands of paying customers. Gamez's path to tech entrepreneurship was unconventional—he didn't own a computer until his twenties, learning programming through a C++ book and paper-and-pencil exercises. He eventually taught himself web development via webmonkey.com tutorials, worked as a ColdFusion developer at a payroll company, and bootstrapped his first SaaS product, Bidsketch, before launching SignWell.

**Starting with Market Research**

The SignWell concept emerged from Bidsketch customers requesting better e-signature solutions. Gamez conducted extensive market validation, discovering widespread complaints about existing tools' expense and complexity. He identified an opportunity to build something with "modern, intuitive UX, that was more affordable, and highly secure and compliant," plus unexplored distribution channels.

**A Wildly Basic MVP**

The initial product performed core functions—uploading and signing documents—but lacked features considered essential at launch, including audit trails, template links, APIs, SOC 2, and HIPAA compliance. Early on, Gamez manually generated audit trail reports on demand while building automated versions. Today, SignWell runs on React, Rails, Redis, Sidekiq, and PostgreSQL on AWS, with Vanta supporting SOC 2 Type II compliance across three categories.

**A Tough Market**

When considering entry, founders warned Gamez not to pursue e-signatures due to market maturity and crowding. He proceeded anyway but recognized that success required both distribution strategy and product differentiation. To learn from dissatisfied competitors' customers, Gamez ran Twitter ads targeting specific audiences, offering Amazon gift cards for ten-minute calls about their experience with existing tools.

**Avoiding Aggressive Pricing Tactics**

SignWell remains one of few e-signature providers offering unlimited documents—a model larger brands abandoned for aggressive pricing and long-term contracts. The platform charges low per-document fees for high-volume API use and is "typically 5x more affordable than competitors." However, affordability alone doesn't ensure success; the company combines competitive pricing with enterprise-grade security and compliance features.

**Indirect Distribution**

Gamez grew traffic to over 100,000 monthly visitors by creating free contract templates—indirect distribution since the company doesn't create contracts but helps sign them. Competitors ignored this adjacent market opportunity. He also implemented a "Dream 100" strategy from "The Ultimate Sales Machine," creating a spreadsheet of important partners and prospects, brainstorming personalized angles, and pursuing relentless follow-up.

**Learn from People in Your Market**

Gamez advises founders to aggressively learn from market participants. He found e-signature startup founders and marketers by identifying launched products and using LinkedIn to contact former employees. He conducted weekly and monthly consulting calls with one sales representative, discussing market-entry strategies and specific challenges.

**Revenue Numbers:**
- Several million in ARR
- Tens of thousands of paying customers
- Over ten million signed documents processed
- >$83K/month

**Key Lessons:**
- MVP minimalism beats perfection: Launching without audit trails validated demand before overbuilding
- Distribution matters more than features in crowded markets
- Indirect distribution outperforms head-to-head competition: Targeting adjacent problems captures primed users
- Deep market learning drives strategy: Engaging with competitors' customers reveals pain points
- Affordability plus compliance wins: Combining competitive pricing with enterprise-grade security
- Doing uncomfortable things accelerates growth: Cold outreach and relentless follow-up compound early traction

**Tech Stack:**
React, Rails, Redis, Sidekiq, PostgreSQL, AWS, Vanta (SOC 2 Type II)
