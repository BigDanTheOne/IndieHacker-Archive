# Telegram's CEO just issued his first public statement since his arrest

**Author:** Channing Allen
**Date:** September 6, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/z7WewABpeWUGNbwkpvi5

---

Pavel Durov, Telegram's CEO, was arrested by French authorities in late August on charges related to insufficient content moderation and his refusal to cooperate with police regarding criminal activity on the platform.

Durov responded via X, asserting that French authorities failed to make genuine contact attempts and should have pursued legal action against the company rather than the individual. He stated: "I was told I may be personally responsible for other people's illegal use of Telegram, because the French authorities didn't receive responses from Telegram."

In his response, Durov highlighted Telegram's stance on privacy versus security trade-offs. He noted the platform has previously left markets with incompatible regulatory demands, referencing Russia's encryption key demands and Iran's request to block protest channels.

## Key Lessons

- Companies may choose to exit markets rather than compromise core principles
- Government coordination methods vary; Durov disputes France's approach was standard practice
- Privacy-focused platforms face mounting pressure from regulators globally
