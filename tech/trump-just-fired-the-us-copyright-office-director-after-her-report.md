# Trump just fired the US Copyright Office director after her report questioned AI training

**Author:** Channing Allen
**Date:** May 12, 2025
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/TxFla0OYbGM569zX0TXH

---

President Trump dismissed U.S. Copyright Office Director Shira Perlmutter by email, occurring just one day after her office released a report examining AI companies' use of copyrighted materials for training purposes.

Perlmutter received termination notification via email without formal explanation. The dismissal followed Trump's removal of Librarian of Congress Carla Hayden two days prior.

The Copyright Office's Part 3 AI copyright study concluded that "making commercial use of vast troves of copyrighted works to produce expressive content that competes with them" exceeds fair use boundaries. The report recommended licensing agreements between copyright holders and AI developers as the preferred solution.

If courts adopt the Copyright Office's position, major AI laboratories including OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI could face increased costs for training data acquisition, mandatory licensing negotiations, slower development timelines, and potential competitive disadvantages for smaller startups. Open-source AI models might encounter challenges accessing diverse training materials.

The American Federation of Musicians characterized Perlmutter's firing as damaging to the copyright community, while music and publishing industries largely supported the report's conclusions.

**Key Lessons:**
- The Copyright Office concluded that commercial use of copyrighted works for AI training exceeds fair use
- Licensing agreements between copyright holders and AI developers may become required
- Smaller startups could face competitive disadvantages if training data costs increase
- Open-source AI models might encounter challenges accessing diverse training materials
