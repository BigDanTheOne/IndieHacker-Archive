# AI has reached the point of diminishing returns. Here's what comes next

**Author:** Darko Gjorgjievski
**Date:** November 15, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/1xNAZFIvtjJweMwZ55Mz

---

The article argues that major AI companies have stopped pursuing model improvements and shifted focus to practical applications. Key points include:

- OpenAI's GPT-5 faces development challenges, Claude's new model is significantly more expensive with minimal improvements, and major companies struggle advancing AI capabilities
- "More use-cases > better AI" represents the strategic pivot
- Companies are releasing AI agents: Claude offers computer automation, OpenAI provides code-reading integration, Google leaked a web-browsing agent
- Two implementation patterns emerge: enhancing prompt context and automating post-response actions
- OpenAI and Claude avoid vertical competition due to API revenue dependency, while Google may pursue niche products given its ad-focused business model

**Key Lessons:**
- Incremental model improvements show diminishing returns
- Integration and automation matter more than raw capability increases
- Business incentives shape product strategy differently across companies

**Tech Stack Mentioned:**
- GPT-5, Claude 3.5 Haiku, Google Gemini
- AI agents, APIs, code editors, web browsers
