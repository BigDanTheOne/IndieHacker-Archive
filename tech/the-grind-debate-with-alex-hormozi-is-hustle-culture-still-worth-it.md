# The grind debate with Alex Hormozi - is hustle culture still worth it?

**Author:** Michal Kankowski
**Date:** December 8, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/mLVnAkiaYUB03DFIYZjA

---

The piece examines whether extreme hustle culture remains viable. Alex Hormozi advocated "no distractions for 10 years," excluding friends' weddings and discretionary activities. Critics including Amanda Goetz countered that "life is not single track success" and built multiple companies while maintaining family presence. Justin Welsh called the philosophy "silly," while Tom Bilyeu offered middle-ground perspective -- individuals should choose their own paths toward success. Daniel Vassallo criticized YC's influence on normalizing unsustainable work (referencing a founder earning $40K annually despite raising millions). Nico abandoned a thriving startup ($7K MRR, 50% growth) due to burnout, selling for $200K instead of waiting for higher returns.

**Revenue Numbers Mentioned:**
- $40K annual salary (referenced YC founder)
- $7K+ monthly recurring revenue (Nico's startup)
- $200K sale price (Nico's exit)
- $9.61/hour equivalent (80-hour workweek calculation)

**Key Lessons:**
- Success definitions vary by individual priorities and life stage
- Extreme sacrifice may not be necessary or healthy
- Burnout risks legitimate profitability
- Balance between ambition and wellbeing increasingly valued
