# Unpopular opinion about Moving back to on-prem

**Author:** Eyal Estrin
**Date:** November 13, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/Nr7ru5meufODSeOJsJXO

---

The piece argues that returning to on-premises infrastructure represents a misconception rather than an emerging trend. Estrin contends that despite analyst reports suggesting organizational migration away from public cloud, actual enterprise success stories documenting such moves remain scarce.

He challenges the premise by noting: "Has the organization embedded cost as part of any architecture decision from day 1, or has the organization ignored cost for a long time?"

## Key Benefits of Public Cloud Highlighted

- **Scalability:** Unlimited horizontal scaling versus hardware-constrained on-premises environments
- **Data Center Management:** Specialists handle infrastructure rather than organizations lacking expertise
- **Hardware Economics:** Pay-per-use eliminates obsolescence risks from upfront purchases
- **Innovation:** Dynamic experimentation with emerging technologies like AI/LLMs
- **Storage Capacity:** Virtually unlimited expansion for petabyte-scale data operations
- **Cloud-Native Development:** Kubernetes and infrastructure-as-code capabilities
- **Data Sovereignty:** Regional deployments satisfy GDPR and residency requirements

**Key Lessons:**
Organizations must adopt strategic cloud adoption through proper cost management, security protocols, sustainability planning, and workforce training to realize public cloud benefits.
