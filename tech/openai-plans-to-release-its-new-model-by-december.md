# OpenAI plans to release its new model by December

**Author:** Stephen Flanders
**Date:** October 25, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/duxmhFtS6PGygj1XRquY

---

According to reporting from The Verge, OpenAI intends to launch its next flagship model, codenamed Orion, by December. Unlike previous releases, the rollout will prioritize enterprise partners, with Microsoft receiving early access through Azure as soon as November.

The reported specifications suggest Orion could be significantly more advanced than GPT-4. Key distinctions include its focus on training-time compute rather than inference-time reasoning, use of synthetic data from the o1 model for training, and potential combination with reasoning models to approach artificial general intelligence capabilities. Training reportedly concluded in September.

However, CEO Sam Altman dismissed the report as "fake news," though he acknowledged that "plenty of great stuff" is forthcoming.

## Key Lessons

- Enterprise-focused AI deployment strategies differ from consumer releases
- Model development timelines and capabilities remain highly speculative without official confirmation
- Strategic partnerships shape AI model distribution approaches

**Tech Stack:** GPT-4, o1 (reasoning model), Orion (new model), Azure
