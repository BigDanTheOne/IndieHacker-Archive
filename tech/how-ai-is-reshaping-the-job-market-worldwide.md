# How AI is reshaping the job market worldwide

**Author:** Michal Kankowski
**Date:** November 20, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/YgV0q5CzjgvGU6avmSzC

---

By 2030, AI is projected to displace 800 million jobs globally. Entry-level and repetitive positions face the greatest threat.

Since late 2022, AI tools like ChatGPT and Midjourney have disrupted labor markets almost immediately. Within eight months of mid-2023, freelance roles susceptible to automation experienced a 21% decline. Graphic design positions fell 17% due to AI image generators. Customer support saw dramatic changes when one company replaced 90% of its support team with chatbot technology, reducing costs by 85% and achieving instant response times.

ChatGPT can perform thousands of tasks across numerous industries. While productivity gains are significant -- consultants using the tool complete work 25% faster with 40% better quality -- junior positions are disappearing fastest across multiple sectors:

- Market research analysts: 53% displacement
- Graphic designers: 50%
- Sales representatives: 67%
- Art director: 24%
- Sales manager: 21%
- Marketing manager: 9%

The most troubling consequence involves the elimination of entry-level roles that traditionally provided mentorship and hands-on learning. Without these foundational positions, emerging professionals cannot develop necessary expertise.

**Key Lessons:**
1. Mentorship Gap Crisis: Removing junior roles eliminates critical skill-building pathways
2. Productivity Paradox: While AI dramatically improves efficiency, it creates workforce preparation challenges
3. Selective Industry Impact: Automation disproportionately affects creative and analytical entry-level work
4. Career Ladder Collapse: The traditional progression from junior to senior positions is deteriorating
