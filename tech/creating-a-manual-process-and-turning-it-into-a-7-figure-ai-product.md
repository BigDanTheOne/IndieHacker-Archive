# Creating a manual process and turning it into a 7-figure AI product

**Author:** James Fleischmann
**Date:** May 29, 2025
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/GO18UdiR1jNiyl3RY0Vu

---

James Layfield, founder of Samplify.ai, transformed enterprise software consolidation from a manual consulting service into an AI-driven product generating over $83K monthly revenue. The company helps large enterprises identify overlapping SaaS tools and redundant licenses, combining AI analysis with structured decision frameworks to recommend consolidation strategies.

Layfield spent months manually reviewing software exports and licensing data before building automation. This hands-on work revealed the specific patterns worth automating. A notable early win was with Volkswagen Financial Services, where over $100M in consolidation opportunities were identified.

Rather than dumping raw data into models, Samplify uses "Monte Carlo-style agent chains" and context injection to ensure reliability, treating AI as a precision tool requiring accountability rather than a black box. The "$15K PoC" offering reduces buyer hesitation by delivering tangible outcomes within 60 days without triggering approval delays.

Layfield emphasizes: "Enterprise buyers don't want more software. They want fewer decisions." Samplify delivers defensible answers backed by logic, not opinions or dashboards. Referrals follow real outcomes: "People don't refer dashboards. They refer the team that saved them seven figures without drama."

The operation runs lean with an AI-leveraged team, no sales infrastructure, no weekly planning calls, and no bloated PM layers. The cofounder brings a background in precision systems (missile guidance). Custom Fortune 500 outputs are delivered in days, not quarters. The company is bootstrapped with no external funding.

**Revenue Numbers:**
- >$83K/month (7-figure ARR)
- Volkswagen Financial Services: $100M+ in consolidation opportunities identified
- $15K proof-of-concept engagements
- No external funding; bootstrapped

**Key Lessons:**
- Start manual before automating: hands-on work reveals the specific patterns worth automating
- LLMs require structure, not magic: use agent chains and context injection for reliability
- Enterprise sales requires low-risk wedges: $15K PoC delivers tangible outcomes within 60 days
- "Enterprise buyers don't want more software. They want fewer decisions."
- "People don't refer dashboards. They refer the team that saved them seven figures without drama."

**Tech Stack:**
GPT-4-turbo, GPT-4o (OpenAI), LangChain with custom orchestration, Python, modular decision paths with real-time context injection
