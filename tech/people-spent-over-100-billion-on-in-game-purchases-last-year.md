# People spent over $100 billion on in-game purchases last year

**Author:** Darko Gjorgjievski
**Date:** November 18, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/VFsMuyccFgTKsvLr7Asb

---

The piece argues that in-game purchases now dwarf actual game sales. The author explains this phenomenon through the lens of "forever games" -- persistent multiplayer worlds like Roblox where players socialize and form identity through cosmetic items.

Primary motivations for spending: Among players aged 16-30, approximately 27% play to socialize compared to 12% of players aged 51+. Virtual cosmetics function similarly to how previous generations defined themselves through cars or concert attendance.

**Revenue Numbers Mentioned:**
- 54 billion GBP spent on game cosmetics last year
- 29.4 billion GBP in direct game sales revenues
- $300 billion valued SaaS market (approximately 3x larger than in-game spending)
- 207.1 million monthly active users on Roblox
- 163 million active players on Among Us

**Key Lessons:**
1. Virtual identity expression drives spending more than gameplay mechanics
2. "Forever games" compete with social media platforms for engagement time
3. Third-party developers create significant value through cosmetic marketplaces
4. Gen Z prioritizes social connection and self-expression in gaming
