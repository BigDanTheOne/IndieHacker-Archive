# How going viral on X helped this solopreneur reach $15k/mo with his native AI apps

**Author:** Katie Hignett
**Date:** September 11, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/ZBbFBKrpwqZQkQsy7Bcs

---

Daniel Nguyen, a 35-year-old solopreneur, built a portfolio of native AI apps reaching $15k/mo in revenue. He emphasizes that "as a solo founder, it's not always about maximizing revenue. We need to optimize for founder happiness too."

## Products

- BoltAI (native macOS AI client) - 7,000+ customers
- PDFPals (chat with PDFs) - ~700 paid customers, $25k total revenue
- KTool (Kindle productivity tool)
- ShotSolve (screenshot AI tool) - 1,700 visitors via lead magnet

## Revenue Numbers

- $10k/month in one-time software license sales
- $5k monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- $25k total revenue from PDFPals

## Key Lessons

- Advocates for the Stair Step Method rather than launching directly into SaaS
- Stresses the importance of charging immediately
- Recommends identifying ideal customer profiles early
- Indie hacking should be "20% learning, 80% doing"
- Execution matters more than understanding theory

## Tech Stack

**Native App:** Swift, SwiftUI, AppKit, SQLite, Sparkle
**Web:** TailwindCSS, NextJS, Cloudflare Pages, Cloudflare Workers, Gitbook
**Services:** Lemon Squeezy, Stripe, Simple Analytics, Bento
