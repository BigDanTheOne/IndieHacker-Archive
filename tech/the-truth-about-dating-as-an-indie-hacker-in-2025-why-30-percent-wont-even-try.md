# The truth about dating as an indie hacker in 2025 - Why 30% won't even try

**Author:** Michal Kankowski
**Date:** December 11, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/AjETRj24R6A7FexnuRnH

---

The article examines dating dynamics within the indie hacker community, presenting survey data showing 60% are in relationships, 10% are single and seeking, and 30% are single and not actively pursuing partners -- contrasting with general US statistics where 30% are single.

Key tensions include unpredictable schedules undermining relationships. One founder noted working 20-hour days and sleeping at the office, ultimately losing his girlfriend without noticing. Another bootstrap attempt resulted in $100k+ losses, marriage decay, and burnout requiring months of recovery.

The piece contrasts online versus offline dating approaches. Survey results indicate 58% of indie hackers favor dating apps, while 42% prefer traditional methods. However, multiple contributors report diminishing returns from apps -- one experienced user described needing approximately 1,000 swipes for a single quality coffee date, citing the experience as "the fastest way to destroy your self-esteem."

Alternative connection methods gaining traction include hobby-based communities (Strava, Letterboxd, running clubs, dance classes) where shared interests create natural meeting points rather than algorithmic matching.

**Revenue Numbers Mentioned:**

**Key Lessons:**
1. Supportive partnerships can enhance entrepreneurial focus rather than compete with it
2. The "24/7 grind" culture creates relationship strain; balance is achievable and beneficial
3. Dating apps show declining effectiveness; community-based connections prove more sustainable
4. Finding partners with similar entrepreneurial backgrounds presents tradeoffs -- shared understanding but compressed quality time
5. Offline meetups through interest groups outperform endless app swiping
