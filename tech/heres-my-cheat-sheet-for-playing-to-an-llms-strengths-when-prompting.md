# Here's my cheat sheet for playing to an LLM's strengths when prompting

**Author:** Courtland Allen
**Date:** April 1, 2025
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/t5W7NuPFBO3TaBiERv0R

---

The article presents a practical guide for effectively using Large Language Models by understanding their capabilities and limitations.

## Key Strengths Identified
- Generating and understanding content
- Transforming and explaining information
- Analyzing patterns and competing concepts
- Interfacing with users

## Key Weaknesses Identified
- **Remembering:** LLMs cannot retain information across separate conversations
- **Iterative reasoning:** Struggles with multi-step logical processes
- **Agency:** Lacks independent initiative and problem-solving
- **Modeling:** Cannot accurately represent complex real-world scenarios

## Key Lessons

1. **Negative prompting matters:** Explicitly stating what NOT to do improves results significantly
2. **Start with goals and backfill:** For multi-step planning, begin with desired outcomes and work backward
3. **Provide external memory:** Architecture systems with persistent context to overcome recall limitations
4. **Treat LLMs as tools, not replacements:** Humans must verify outputs and maintain decision-making authority
5. **Break complex tasks into steps:** Decompose requests rather than asking for comprehensive solutions at once
