# Validating via services before building a $27k MRR product

**Author:** James Fleischmann
**Date:** January 15, 2026
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/7FrT6FXChPOiUK4PbXFp

---

Roman Czerny, a 30-year-old founder based in Lisbon, Portugal, shares his journey building Gojiberry AI, an intent-based LinkedIn outreach tool. After selling his first SaaS product (CocoAI) for seven figures, he identified a winning growth tactic and decided to productize it.

**Background:** Czerny began his entrepreneurial journey in 2019 as a freelancer, then moved into affiliate marketing before discovering his passion for SaaS in 2023.

**The Discovery:** His previous e-commerce SaaS revealed that founders actively engaging on LinkedIn around specific topics converted significantly better. Rather than build immediately, the team manually searched LinkedIn for qualified prospectsâ€”a process that often took 5+ hours to find 100 leads but proved highly effective.

**Manual Validation Phase:** Instead of writing code initially, Czerny spent an entire summer manually performing lead research with virtual assistants based in Pakistan and India. This painful, unscalable process served a critical purpose: proving genuine customer demand and satisfaction existed before any product development.

**Product Development:** The team later automated this proven manual process using AWS, Framer, Firebase, Gemini, Claude, and ChatGPT.

**Growth Strategy:** Rather than relying on a single channel, Czerny employed a multi-dimensional approach:
- Reddit for building early trust within SaaS communities
- LinkedIn through viral content combined with proprietary outbound campaigns
- Cold email at scale
- Strategic influencer partnerships with established B2B voices
- YouTube tutorials, newsletter sponsorships, and webinars

**Revenue Numbers:**
- $27,000 MRR at beginning of December
- $35,000 in sales during December 2025
- Previous exit (CocoAI): Seven-figure acquisition price

**Key Lessons:**
- "To build the MVP, we did not write a single line of product code. Instead, we first focused on getting customers."
- Founder-market fit matters: leverage the exact growth methodology that powered previous success
- Consistency over silver bullets: "showing up everywhere, every single day"
- ICP narrowing: Initially attempting to serve multiple customer profiles diluted effectiveness
- Outcome-driven results: prioritize measurable business outcomes over vanity metrics
- Built-in expansion revenue occurs naturally as teams grow

**Tech Stack:**
AWS, Framer, Firebase, Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT
