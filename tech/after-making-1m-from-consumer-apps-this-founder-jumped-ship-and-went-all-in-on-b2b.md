# After making $1M+ from consumer apps, this founder jumped ship and went all in on B2B AI SaaS

## Revenue Numbers

- Previous consumer apps: **$1M+**
- CreatorKit current ARR: **Six figures** (>$100K)
- CreatorKit monthly revenue: **>$8.5K/month**

## Key Lessons

1. **Focus Over Expansion** - "You can do ANYTHING, but not EVERYTHING." The founder initially over-built features, delaying the MVP and allowing competitors to emerge.

2. **Shipping Velocity Matters** - Quick iteration combined with focus produces better results than perfectionism. The phrase "done is better than perfect" becomes an excuse for mediocrity without genuine focus.

3. **Singular Marketing Channel** - Rather than pursuing all growth strategies simultaneously, identify one primary channel per growth stage.

4. **Defensible Products** - Consumer apps lack moats; B2B SaaS with proprietary technology (custom AI models) provides sustainable competitive advantage.

5. **Work-Life Imbalance Is Normal** - Building ambitious products requires accepting extended periods of imbalance before achieving equilibrium.

6. **Learning Through Action** - Direct experience produces more valuable insights than courses or theory; ask advisors slightly ahead of your stage.

## Full Article

Kevin Natanzon spent 10+ years building startups. After creating consumer apps used by 15 million people and generating $1M+ in revenue, he recognized the App Store model had become unsustainable. Publisher-based acquisition channels on Twitter and Instagram collapsed following platform purges, eliminating his primary growth mechanism.

His previous apps lacked defensibility—easily replicated products dependent on App Store Optimization (ASO) rather than genuine differentiation. This recognition prompted him to pivot entirely.

### The Genesis of CreatorKit

During his consumer app years, Natanzon spent countless hours creating marketing videos in After Effects, rendering 30-second clips repeatedly. This pain point inspired CreatorKit—an AI video platform designed for rapid marketing video creation. Rather than building the tool from scratch, he reinvested profits into this new venture.

CreatorKit offers tiered pricing: PRO and Business plans, each providing access to different AI actors and varying monthly video generation quotas. The platform has grown to six-figure annual recurring revenue.

### Strategic Pivots

Initially, Natanzon pursued a broad video platform approach, building extensive timelines and features prematurely. Multiple decisions defaulted to "yes," resulting in an oversized MVP. Simultaneously, numerous competitors emerged offering similar general-purpose video solutions.

Recognizing the crowded landscape, he pivoted focus exclusively toward AI avatar technology—differentiating CreatorKit as specialized AI video creation infrastructure. This specialization triggered rapid growth following AI actor launches.

### Technical Differentiation

CreatorKit's competitive advantage stems from proprietary AI technology. Most video platforms utilize identical lipsync APIs, producing uncanny, artificial results. Natanzon's team developed custom video restoration models (based on GPFGAN architecture), enabling hyperrealistic AI actors unavailable elsewhere.

This custom infrastructure introduced hosting complexity unsuitable for standard elastic GPU solutions like SageMaker. His cofounder Carlos managed this technical challenge—a capability not easily outsourced or replicated.

### Shipping and Focus

Natanzon emphasizes shipping velocity as critical for startup success, though "done is better than perfect" often justified mediocre releases. True acceleration requires laser-focused execution on singular capabilities rather than breadth.

Slower shipping resulted from attempting too many initiatives simultaneously. Paradoxically, constraints accelerate shipping velocity by eliminating decision paralysis.

### Marketing Channel Concentration

Early growth relied on SEO paired with social media (TikTok and Instagram). The company hired a full-time creator as brand face, generating viral organic content and accumulating 80K TikTok and 40K Instagram followers.

When this creator departed, engagement collapsed—exposing dependency on individual personalities. Modern AI avatars solve this vulnerability; digital actors remain perpetually available, preventing brand loss through personnel changes.

### Work-Life Dynamics

Natanzon rejects popular "work-life balance" narratives, noting that building ambitious products requires extended imbalance periods. He cites Jeff Bezos's famous "work-life harmony" quote while noting Bezos's subsequent divorce and substantial wealth loss—suggesting harmony language obscures underlying intensity.

Rather than seeking immediate balance, he advocates planning for eventual equilibrium after concentrated building phases.

### Foundational Advice

Direct action generates more learning than courses or education. "Action produces information"—avoiding analysis paralysis matters more than theoretical preparation.

Optimal guidance comes from mentors slightly ahead in their entrepreneurial journey, not dramatically advanced figures. Reciprocally, helping those behind you strengthens ecosystem health.
