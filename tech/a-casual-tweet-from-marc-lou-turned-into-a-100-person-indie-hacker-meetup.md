# A casual tweet from Marc Lou turned into a 100-person indie hacker meetup

**Author:** Michal Kankowski
**Date:** January 8, 2025
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/WDDjOWfI4fHEqtiVaC4s

---

Marc Lou's spontaneous invitation on social media evolved into an unexpected gathering of 100+ indie hackers at Jardin du Luxembourg in Paris on January 5, 2025. The organizer anticipated roughly 10 attendees but witnessed significant oversubscription, with participants traveling internationally to attend.

## Key Statistics from the Meetup

- 90% of attendees had generated at least $1 in online revenue
- 50% operated startups generating sustainable income
- Several participants flew internationally to participate

## Notable Success Stories

Erikas Malisauskas achieved $3.4M revenue in 2024 through Shopify applications, relying entirely on organic recommendations rather than paid marketing channels.

## Key Lessons Shared

1. "Quality > Quantity"
2. Prioritize in-person networking
3. Imposter syndrome affects many founders
4. Building personal brand matters
5. Consistent shipping generates opportunities
6. Learn from both ahead and behind peers
7. Parallel income streams alongside employment are common
8. Launch without waiting for "perfect" ideas
9. Seek community through platforms like WhatsApp groups
10. Meeting successful figures humanizes their achievements

## Additional Meetups

A three-day indie hacker gathering in Kerala, India drew 28 participants organized by serial founder Ayush Chaturvedi in December 2024.
