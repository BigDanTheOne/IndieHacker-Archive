## Revenue Numbers

- Current: $900,000/month (~$10.8M annually)
- 2024 total: $4.5M
- Initial launch revenue: $3,000
- Early launches: "a few hundred" dollars each
- User base: 1.3M+ users
- Photos generated: 30M+

## Key Lessons

### Launch Strategy
- "Launch early. Most founders don't launch early enough. Our first landing page was terrible, but if we hadn't launched when we did, things could be different"
- Move rapidly when adopting new technologies
- Keep expenses low while maintaining strong margins

### Growth Tactics
- Guerrilla marketing (Reddit posts about personal success with the product)
- Building audience on X and LinkedIn
- Affiliate marketing programs (used Rewardful)
- Conversion rate optimization through A/B testing
- What didn't work: Cold emails, Meta ads, PR outreach

### Team and Operations
- Hiring is the hardest challenge
- Avoid agencies and part-time contractors
- Learn roles before hiring for them
- Constant iteration on hiring processes

### Personal Discipline
- Maintain consistent routine and exercise
- Adequate sleep and scheduled downtime
- Cultivate supportive relationships
- Balance work with stable personal life

## Tech Stack

### Core Technologies
- React, Next.js, TypeScript
- PostgreSQL database
- Heroku, AWS infrastructure
- GraphQL
- Gradio (for prototype)

### AI/ML Foundation
- Stable Diffusion (open-source text-to-image model)
- DreamBooth algorithm
- GPU infrastructure costs mentioned as primary expense

## Product Details

### Pricing Model
- Professional headshots: $35-$75 (depending on package)
- AI photo editor: $15/month ($129/year subscription)
- AI photo generator: $29/month subscription
- Custom enterprise contracts available

### Value Proposition
Eliminates need for expensive photoshoots (typically $250+) by generating hyperrealistic images with customizable outfits and backgrounds.

## Founder Background

Wesley Tian previously worked at early-stage YC-backed startups, studied competitive machine learning, and applied to OpenAI in 2017. He launched with $15k-$20k in personal savings and built initial momentum by creating 12 apps with waitlists to test market demand.
