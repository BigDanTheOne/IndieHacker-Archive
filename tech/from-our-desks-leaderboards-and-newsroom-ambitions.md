# From Our Desks - Leaderboards and Newsroom Ambitions

**Author:** Courtland Allen, Katie Hignett, Stephen Flanders, Channing Allen, Darko Gjorgjievski, James Fleischmann
**Date:** November 3, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/GvYMphGS3Vba1g0O2UDM

---

This weekly column features insights from the Indie Hackers team across six perspectives:

**Channing Allen** discusses scaling the newsroom from three to five newsletters weekly. Despite getting ill, the team autonomously coordinated efforts without micromanagement, successfully hitting their milestone while maintaining quality journalism.

**Courtland Allen** explains pivoting their product leaderboard from a launch-based system to content-driven updates. He argues "launch-based leaderboards" lack recurring traffic potential, whereas a "build in public" model better serves the community by allowing product page posts to populate daily leaderboards.

**Katie Hignett** reflects on X's API pricing increases to $200/month, noting how her early coding projects relied on affordable access. She also reconsidered her app usage after reading that professional social media engagement should remain on laptops only.

**Stephen Flanders** shares his WordPress plugin experience, warning that building on WordPress involves slow platforms, lengthy approval processes, and ongoing ecosystem drama. He recommends avoiding the platform entirely.

**Darko Gjorgjievski** highlights how email marketing best practices evolve constantly, citing analysis showing which cold email approaches achieve high response rates. He notes coverage of 49 unicorn startups and 100 top creators in 2024.

**James Fleischmann** interviews founders who left corporate roles due to meaninglessness, emphasizing that "impact" matters more than freedom or income for driving entrepreneurial pursuits.

**Key Lessons:**
1. Team autonomy enables scaling operations during crises
2. Launch-based leaderboards lack sustainable business viability
3. Building on WordPress presents significant technical and procedural obstacles
4. Email marketing strategies require constant evolution
5. Visible work impact drives entrepreneurial motivation more than financial incentives
