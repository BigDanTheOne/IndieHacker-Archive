# Building a $100M ARR portfolio of products

**Author:** James Fleischmann
**Date:** December 19, 2025
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/erendy8MDnGpBEP0F2GN

---

Tim Schumacher founded saas.group, a holding company for independent SaaS brands. He began his entrepreneurial journey in his teenage years, starting with coding and eventually building Sedo.com, a domain marketplace he operated for ten years.

After becoming an angel investor and founding multiple companies, Schumacher identified three key problems: talented founders experienced burnout or scaling challenges despite having quality products; acquisitions often failed because acquirers misunderstood how to operate the businesses; and he discovered he excelled at scaling rather than building from scratch.

These insights led to saas.group's foundation—creating a trusted, long-term home for independent SaaS products. The company now operates over 25 brands across approximately 400 people in 30 countries, generating $100M in annual recurring revenue (ARR).

**Notable Portfolio Companies:**
- GitTower.com—a graphical Git client
- ScraperAPI.com—developer-friendly web scraping tool
- Ayrshare.com—social media connectivity for developers
- Prerender.io—website visibility enhancement for search engines and AI

**Operating Model as the Product:**
Rather than treating individual SaaS products as the primary product, saas.group's actual product is its acquisition and operating model. The company typically acquires SaaS businesses generating between $2-$5 million ARR. Several portfolio companies contribute mid- to high-seven-figure ARR individually.

The operating system consists of small, autonomous teams paired with centralized support services for marketing, DevOps, and finance. This structure applies consistently across all acquisitions.

**Founder-Friendly Philosophy:**
The company maintains a reputation as the most founder-friendly acquirer in the space. This is achieved through transparency, honoring agreements, and preserving brand independence. Word-of-mouth from founders, brokers, and investors generates steady acquisition opportunities—demonstrating that reputation functions as a genuine competitive moat.

**Product Evolution After Acquisition:**
- AddSearch.com (website search tool): Reinvented itself as "AI Answers," an AI-based answer engine, successfully reviving declining revenue.
- Keyword.com (SEO monitoring): Expanded beyond traditional keyword monitoring to track brand visibility across AI search platforms including ChatGPT, AI Mode, Perplexity, and Gemini.
- Prerender.com: Achieved an 80% reduction in AWS infrastructure costs post-acquisition.

**Revenue Numbers:**
- $100M ARR (total portfolio)
- ~$8.3M monthly revenue
- $2-$5 million ARR (typical acquisition range)
- Mid- to high-seven figures (individual company contributions)
- 80% AWS cost reduction (Prerender.com example)
- 400 employees across 30 countries

**Key Lessons:**
- Operating model as product: The acquisition playbook itself is the core product
- Execution beats innovation: Consistent, disciplined execution outperforms flashy growth hacks
- Founder-friendly reputation creates moat through word-of-mouth
- Small autonomous teams outperform large orgs when given autonomy and central support
- Product reinvention prevents decline: Recognizing market shifts and pivoting sustains revenue
- Bootstrap DNA drives efficiency: Founders with bootstrapper mentality create frugal, focused operations

**Tech Stack:**
No single enforced stack. Bias toward simplicity and maintainability with developer-driven decisions. Portfolio includes GitTower, ScraperAPI, Ayrshare, Prerender—each optimized for their specific product needs.
