# Does over-scheduling or going with the flow work best for indie hackers?

**Author:** Michal Kankowski
**Date:** February 19, 2025
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/5cPc0jLnsBGGpa1kr1N6

---

The piece examines two contrasting productivity philosophies among successful entrepreneurs: meticulous scheduling versus flexible, intuition-based approaches.

## The Manager's Mindset (Structured Scheduling)

**Leila Hormozi** manages a $500M portfolio using organized yet flexible scheduling. Her approach involves listing weekly projects, ranking priorities, blocking break time, scheduling tasks, and reserving buffer time.

**Rob Dyrdek** ($350M wealth) plans his entire life on a calendar -- mornings, date nights, family time, and meetings -- while maintaining flexibility based on mood.

**Elon Musk** divides his day into 5-minute increments, creating 216 slots within an 18-hour day.

## The Maker's Mindset (Flow-Based Approach)

**John Rush** relies on memory rather than systems. He manages approximately 30 businesses without project management software.

**Shaan Puri** (My First Million podcast) asks one morning question: "What's the one thing or outcome I'm going to work on that if I just did that one thing today would be a win?"

**Pat Walls** (Starter Story) blocks deep work time but uses Apple Notes for checklists.

## Revenue Numbers Mentioned

- Leila Hormozi: $500M portfolio (age 30)
- Rob Dyrdek: $350M wealth
- Pat Walls: $1M business

## Key Lessons

1. **Intentionality matters most** -- Success depends on deliberate choices, not the system itself
2. **No universal formula exists** -- Personalities and work types require different approaches
3. **The effectiveness-vs-efficiency distinction** -- Doing fewer important things often beats doing many things efficiently
4. **Seasons change requirements** -- Early-stage projects may need different systems than scaling operations
