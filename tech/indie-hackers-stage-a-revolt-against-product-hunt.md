# Indie hackers stage a revolt against Product Hunt

**Author:** Stephen Flanders
**Date:** September 30, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/8GZt0khoc1B5EguEolfQ

---

The indie hacker community staged a significant uprising against Product Hunt following the non-featuring of Nico Jeannen's Landing Analyze product. Jeannen's critical tweet sparked widespread frustration about what many perceive as Product Hunt's decline and favoritism toward VC-backed startups.

## Key Criticisms

- **Tibo (2022 Maker of the Year):** Characterized Product Hunt as merely "an SEO tool" offering little beyond directory backlinks
- **Adam Lyttle:** Compared listing on Product Hunt to appearing in outdated phone directories
- **Martin Donadieu:** Called the platform "a joke" and urged makers to migrate to alternatives like Uneed and BetaList
- **Damon Chen:** Identified overly subjective curation criteria as problematic

## CEO Response

Rajiv Ayyangar defended the curation process, arguing that Jeannen's landing page analyzer lacked the novelty of featured products. He emphasized that reducing 200+ daily launches requires editorial judgment and that guidelines remain publicly available.

## Alternative Platforms Emerging

Several developers announced competing platforms:
- MicroLaunch (Said Aitmbarek)
- Launching.today (Yossi Segev)
- LaunchDay (Dagobert Renouf)
- Fazier (Falak Sher)

Indie Hackers itself signaled potential involvement in this space.

## Key Lessons

1. Platform decay occurs when communities prioritize monetization over user value
2. Transparent curation rules matter more than opaque editorial decisions
3. Community-driven voting systems may outperform human moderators
4. Market fragmentation benefits independent builders
