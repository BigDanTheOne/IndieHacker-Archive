# Bitcoin hits $100,000 for the first time

**Author:** Stephen Flanders
**Date:** December 6, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/1WO94YfqHz0S0wt1OmTe

---

The cryptocurrency recently achieved a six-figure valuation for the first time in history. Two years prior, bitcoin traded at $15,000 while the industry grappled with significant fraud. On Wednesday, the asset reached $103,000 at its peak, though it subsequently pulled back to $97,500.

The surge represents recovery from a period of uncertainty. Two key catalysts drove this momentum. First, spot exchange-traded funds enabled mainstream investment through traditional stock markets, attracting substantial institutional capital. Second, political developments shifted regulatory sentiment favorably toward digital assets.

The trajectory stalled for 236 days after reaching $73,000, creating uncertainty about further gains. However, appointments of pro-cryptocurrency regulators and promises of government bitcoin purchases accelerated the recent rally.

Current market indicators suggest elevated enthusiasm. The Coinbase application now ranks 13th among most-downloaded apps, compared to 46th previously. Bitcoin dominance (BTC.D) decreased from 60% to 55%, indicating investor interest in alternative cryptocurrencies.

**Key Lessons:**
- Market catalysts matter significantly for asset valuation
- Regulatory environment influences cryptocurrency adoption
- Market indicators like app rankings and dominance ratios provide insights into cycle timing
