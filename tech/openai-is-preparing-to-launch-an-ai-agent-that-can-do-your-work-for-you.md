# OpenAI is preparing to launch an AI agent that can do your work for you

**Author:** Stephen Flanders
**Date:** November 14, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/xwve8gin67RkBdaSj6MD

---

OpenAI is nearing the launch of "Operator," their first autonomous AI agent, reportedly coming in January. This agent would use your computer on your behalf to perform tasks like writing code, booking restaurant reservations, purchasing plane tickets, and sending emails.

During OpenAI's Reddit AMA, CEO Sam Altman described agents as "the next giant breakthrough." According to OpenAI's internal metrics, agents represent the next evolutionary step toward artificial general intelligence (AGI).

The announcement provides encouragement to AI researchers concerned about the "scaling law" plateau -- the observation that AI improvements are slowing with additional training data. Agents could temporarily address this concern and potentially represent AI's next major breakthrough.

The article notes that Anthropic recently released Claude with Computer Use capabilities, indicating multiple companies are pursuing similar autonomous agent technologies.

**Key Lessons:**
- Autonomous agents may overcome current AI scaling limitations
- Multiple organizations are pursuing similar agent-based approaches
- Agents could accelerate progress toward AGI
