# Companies are leveraging Gen Z's Brain Rot trend in their marketing

**Author:** Michal Kankowski
**Date:** December 10, 2024
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/ufNOsSPwDK5uvgwWGcMO

---

The piece discusses how brands are adopting "brain rot" humor -- absurdist, goofy content consumed in excess on social media -- as a marketing strategy. Oxford University Press named it 2024's Word of the Year, with usage growing 230% annually.

Duolingo and Nutter Butter exemplify successful implementations. Duolingo's owl singing gibberish to "Cotton Eye Joe" garnered 68 million views; Nutter Butter's quirky video reached 11 million views. The trend evolved from deep-fried memes and shitposting, emphasizing childlike chaos and irony.

However, experts caution moderation. Marketing strategist Maggie Walsh warns that "If brain rot takes over your whole strategy, you're basically giving your brand voice an expiration date." Oxford researchers note Gen Z uses brain rot content as stress relief amid financial and mental health pressures.

The article concludes that while brain rot generates engagement, brands must balance it with meaningful value to avoid brand damage.

**Key Lessons:**
- Adopt brain rot humor selectively for Gen Z/Gen Alpha audiences
- Balance chaotic content with authentic brand value
- Excessive reliance on trends risks brand credibility
