# Hitting $500k ARR in two years by staying close to her customers

**Author:** James Fleischmann
**Date:** May 15, 2025
**Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/S0ugOp2P1tjxWacN3A7V

---

Tham (Sylvia) Nguyen and her cofounder launched MindPal over a weekend in August 2023 while on college summer break, aiming to improve upon simple "chat with PDF" applications by allowing users to interact with multiple file types (Word docs, websites, presentations, videos).

The product evolved from a one-page file-chat tool to a custom AI agent builder (September 2023), then expanded to a multi-agent workflow platform (February 2024). MindPal now positions itself as a no-code platform for building AI agents and automation systems.

Nguyen reduced dependency on external AI frameworks (LangChain, Vercel AI SDK) by developing proprietary infrastructure. She implemented internal use of MindPal to identify operational improvements and adopted a systems-thinking approach to scale operations beyond team capacity.

Growth channels include customer referrals and an affiliate program (20% recurring commission), community building (Facebook group with ~3k members and external partnerships), content marketing (YouTube videos, blog posts, practical templates), free tools (standalone mini-tools like a free AI Agent Builder), and a Product Hunt launch (achieved Top 4 Product of the Day).

Early price increases from $12 to $49+ attracted serious, qualified customers with better retention. Regular product iteration based on direct user feedback shaped all major pivots. Nguyen and cofounder Tuan previously spent 18 months on a failed startup that lacked customer connection--this failure became their most valuable education.

**Revenue Numbers:**
- $500,000 ARR
- $42,000 monthly revenue
- Less than 2 years from launch (August 2023) to current
- Initial pricing: $12/month
- Current pricing: $49-$449/month

**Key Lessons:**
- "Stay close to your customers" -- customer proximity trumps most other business considerations
- Early price increases attract serious, qualified customers with better retention
- Using your own product internally builds deeper product understanding
- System-level thinking drives compounding growth better than linear hiring
- Building products people actually want is the critical learning from previous failures
- Reduce dependency on external frameworks by developing proprietary infrastructure

**Tech Stack:**
Proprietary AI agent and workflow infrastructure (replaced LangChain, Vercel AI SDK), Cursor (AI coding assistant)
